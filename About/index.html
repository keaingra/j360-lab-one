Lin-Manuel Miranda was born on January 16, 1980 in New York City in the Manhattan neighborhood of Inwood. His parents are Luis A. Miranda, Jr., a political consultant, and Luz Towns-Miranda, a clinical psychologist, both hailing from Puerto Rico.

From a very young age, Lin showed a gift for music and performing. He would perform with his elementary school's choir and also took piano lessons alongside his siter Luz.

He attended Hunter College Elementary School, where his love of music grew under the instruction of music teacher Barbara Ames. He then attended Hunter College High School from grades 7-12 and graduated in 1998.

Lin then went to Wesleyan University in Connecticut for college, where he co-founded an improv comedy rap troupe called Freestyle Love Supreme. He also performed in theatrical productions while there, even taking on the title role in Jesus Christ Superstar. It was also at Wesleyan where he layed the foundation for his career as one of the best composers on Broadway today.

The initial draft of Lin's first musical In the Heights was written in 1999 during Lin's sophomore year. The show was then accepted by Wesleyan's student theatre company and ran from April 20-22. Lin continued to write, direct, and perform in shows during his time there until he graduated in 2002.

After graduating, Lin began working with John Buffalo Mailer and director Thomas "Tommy" Kail on various drafts of In the Heights. He also worked as an English teacher at Hunter College High at this time.

In the Heights, now featuring music and lyrics by Lin and a book by Quiara Hudes, then played at the Eugene O'Neill Theater Center in Connecticut in 2005. The show then opened Off-Broadway at at 37 Arts Theater February 2007 and closed in July of that year.

It was at this time that Lin read the book that would give him the inspiration for his next show: Ron Chernow's biography on Alexander Hamilton. Lin then checked to see if anyone had already made a musical out of Hamilton's life, and finding none, began working on The Hamilton Mixtape.

The show had its Broadway premiere at the Richard Rodgers Theatre, with previews opening on February 14, 2008. The official opening was March 9 of the same year.

Heights went on to win four Tony Awards, including Best Musical. Lin also won for Best Score and Andy Blankenbuehler won for Best Choreography. Music Director Alex Lacaimore and Bill Sherman won for Best Orchestrations. The show then went on to win the Grammy Award for Best Musical Show Album.

On May 12, 2009, Lin debuted the first number from The Hamilton Mixtape at the White House Evening of Poetry, Music, and the Spoken Word.

On September 5, 2010, Lin married his partner Vanessa Nadal.

In the Heights had its final Broadway performance on January 9, 2011.

That same month, Lin's next show Bring It On opened in Atlanta. It then toured across the country and played at the St. James Theatre on Broadway in 2012. It was nominated for two Tony Awards.

In 2013, the first reading of Hamilton was held at Vassar College.

In 2014 Lin appeared as Jon in the Encores production of Tick, Tick...Boom!.

Lin's life changed once again on November 10, 2014, when Vanessa gave birth to their son Sebastian.

Hamilton opened Off-Broadway at the Public Theater on January 20, 2015 and eneded its run on May 3. It was announced that the show would transfer to Broadway that summer.

The show opened at the Richard Rodgers Theatre on August 6, 2015 and is still playing the venue.

Lin announced via Twitter that he is one of the songwriters for Disney's 56th animated musical Moana. He also wrote the music for the cantina scene in Star Wars, Episode VII: The Force Awakens.

Hamilton won the Grammy for Best Musical Theater Album on February 15, 2016 after performing on the show live from the Richard Rodgers.
